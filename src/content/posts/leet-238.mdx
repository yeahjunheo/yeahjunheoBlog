## Two-pass solution FTW

With a quick look at the question, I was going to run a single-pass with a variable to hold the
total product of the array. That way, I could simply divide for each element of the array and
return the values. An easy solution! But the question strictly states to not use division
operations.

With that, the next thought was to run, if possible, a minimal amount of passes through the array.
If I went through each element, one-by-one, to find each product, it'll take $O(n^2)$ times to
a final array. That's not optimal! So taking the <mark>$O(1)$ extra space</mark> as a clue, I on
a two-pass solution, holding onto the current product during each pass, and multiplying to the
next element.

This means for an array `[1,2,3,4]`, at 0-idx, we'll hold onto $1$. Then at 1-idx, we'll multiply
the current element with $1$ in our `answer` array `[1,1,1,1]`, and then multiple $1$ with $2$ to
hold onto $2$. Then for the next, we do the same and hold onto $6$, and so on. Then after our first
pass, we'll have `answer = [1,1,2,6]`. Then we go from the right and repeat our process until
0-idx, and we'll have `[24,12,8,6]`.

## Performance evaluation

With our two-pass solution, we have a time complexity of $O(n)$ (very beautiful) and a space
complexity of $O(n)$ (coming from the `answer` array). Overall very quick and satisfying solution.
